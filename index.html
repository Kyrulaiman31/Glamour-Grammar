Why we need to learn tenses?

Welcome to our humble abode where you can improve your grammar by learning tense through comprehensive lessons and fun activities but why tense? Well, It's important to remember that English is a chronological language. English speakers merely need to know when a specific action or event occurred. English speakers use verb tenses to express timeline, or the order in which events and dates occur. Each tense denotes a link between two or more time periods or the precise time of an event, emphasising the significance of English grammar tenses. They can be utilised to produce multiple interpretations from the same verbs, assisting the listener in comprehending the content of your story. As a result, English includes a large number of verb tenses, some of which show a relationship between two time periods and others which reflect the precise timing of an activity. Furthermore, the breadth of English tenses should never be overlooked. We can indicate very different ideas by merely changing the tense; we can even use tenses as rhetorical techniques to emphasise whether we passionately believe a project "will succeed" or simply express that we "will see" if it "will succeed." Tenses and the chronological order of events and acts are less important in other languages. Instead, they emphasise the (hierarchical) ties between the persons involved in the events and actions that the speaker/narrator is describing. Japanese is an example of a language that places a greater focus on interpersonal ties. As a result, English learners who speak Japanese as their first language should pay special attention to the tenses in English and use visualisation tools to help them move English tenses from the relatively abstract domain of textbooks to a much more palpable and understandable medium. For example, why not take a wooden box and imagine that the past tenses need to placed within that box to symbolize how they describe events and actions that started and were completed in the past. This method works for tactile and visual learners alike. Let's go over the different types of tenses if you're still not sure why verb tenses are significant. After all, the purpose of this website is to help you grasp the tenses and demonstrate how much fun it is to embark on this adventure!
